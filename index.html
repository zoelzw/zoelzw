I am Zoe, a researcher in
Human Computer Interaction
based in Chicago.



I am keen to seek out new guiding principles and concepts that seamlessly integrate groundbreaking technologies and techniques with human natural dexterity beyond current limitations.

My primary research goal is to build a computation foundation and engineering framework that enables seamless integration and co-adaption of computational tools and users to assist and augment human creativity and experiences.

I plan to achieve this vision by exploring new paradigms in HCI and developing novel interactive devices, enabling technologies and fabrication tools that bring independence and freedom in both the digital and physical world to diverse audiences.

I am a graduating master student in Computer Science at the University of Chicago, and my latest work with my colleagues in the Human Computer Integration Lab won the Best Paper Award from ACM UIST. Previously, I studied Electrical Engineering with a concentration in Control Systems at NC State University and conducted series of research in Solarpack and Neuramuscular Rehabiliation Engineering Lab
